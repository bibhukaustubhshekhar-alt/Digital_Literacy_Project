STAY SAFE ONLINE: STUDENT CHECKLIST
Use Strong, Unique Passwords: Create complex passwords for your university portals and email. Avoid using your name, birthdate, or "123456."

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Always turn on 2FA (via SMS or an authenticator app) for your Google, LinkedIn, and Instagram accounts to prevent hacking.

Verify UPI "Request Money" Scams: Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Remember: A PIN is only required to send money or check your balance.

Secure Your UPI Apps: Use a separate screen lock or biometric lock for apps like PhonePe, Google Pay, or Paytm to prevent unauthorized access if your phone is misplaced.

Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks: Do not log into bank accounts or fill out exam forms using free campus or cafe Wi-Fi. Use your mobile data or a trusted VPN instead.

Beware of "Job" or "Task" Scams: Be skeptical of WhatsApp or Telegram messages offering easy money for liking YouTube videos or rating hotels. These are common recruitment scams.

Check URLs Before Clicking: Before entering credentials on a college or scholarship portal, ensure the URL starts with https:// and look for the padlock icon.

Limit Personal Info on Social Media: Do not post photos of your Aadhaar card, college ID, or hall tickets, as these contain sensitive data used for identity theft.

HOW TO REPORT CYBERCRIME IN INDIA
If you face any financial fraud, harassment, or identity theft, report it immediately:

Official Website: cybercrime.gov.in (National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal)

National Helpline Number: 1930 (Call this immediately for financial/UPI fraud to freeze the transaction)
